Select 1st Preference: Investment Asset classes


Australian shares as the name suggests will provide an investor access to the top 200 shares mainly on the Australian share market. In reality a heavy concentration towards the Top 20 - 50 shares is more likely.
Investment into Listed Property, will provide greater access to capital as you will hold a mixture of commercial, office, retail, warehouse, distribution type properites via listed companies on the Australian and other share markets.
Taking up International Share investment provides the greatest scope since the Australian share market really only represents around 2% of all world equity markets. On top of that, since Australia is not a tech heavy, or research heavy nation, it is imperative to hold International shares in order to get greater diversification.
Bonds are known as fixed income investments that involve the receipt of interest payments from a large corporation or government. The investor essentially lends money in the anticipation of getting the capital back at a later date, and in the meantime receives an interest rate return, dictated by the level of risk being undertaken.
Commodity investment is often seen as more speculative and something that should not be a massive part of your investment portfolio. You may believe gold, or lithium, or some precious metal for instance will rise in value. Since you may not receive an income from this asset, this is why it is considered speculative.
