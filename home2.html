Sports


My two nicknames growing up were "Sport", and "Iceman". My Mum coined the first term, as I was the youngest of 6, and she could never get me to come in for supper since I would be playing with my siblings/neighbours on the cul-de-sac where we lived.

The second nickname was from my basketball coach, Mr March. in the winter of 1982, when he saw me hit some tough free throws in a close game, and commented: "you must have ice in your veins".

My Computer Science journey


Computers have always intrigued me, and I desired to take a computer science class in Grade 9 (Junior High school in Canada). I was told by my guidance counsellor that I would need to take up a typing class if I was also going to take computer science in my Grade 9 year.
Little did I know that I would end up being 1 of 2 boys in that typing class, just as I was becoming a teen. Suffice to say I enjoyed typing...maybe even more than Computer science! 😉

That summer my Dad and sister and I purchased a Commodore VIC20 computer, see image below. As a 13 year old I was so excited about this. We didn't do too much programming on that VIC20 but I fell in love with it, and played certain games on it mostly.
This was an 8 bit home computer that we bought in the summer of 1981 and I believe that they were discontinued around 4-5 years later.
It had an atari joystick port, and since I delivered newspapers...not an easy thing to do in Winnipeg, Canada in the winter ❄, I saved up and bought a beautiful black and red joystick - the image above. Prior to that we had to just bash away on various keys!
The first game I ever bought was called Gorf, a fixed shooter game, and I spent many hours with my friends playing that game.

Some of the other games I played were

  • Dig Dug - a maze arcade game
  • Red Alert - a war based fixed shooter game
  • Donkey Kong - a well known game that spanned many generations, cultures, and genres
